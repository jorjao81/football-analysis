The shape of the funnel

Everyone qualifies from somewhere. Almost no one wins from outside two places.

Across the modern 32-team era (1998–2022, seven tournaments) the pool that qualifies is genuinely global — but the deeper the round, the more the map collapses onto Europe and South America.

Semi-final berths, 1982–2026 (44 slots played)

Of every semi-finalist across twelve World Cups, all but two came from UEFA or CONMEBOL. The exceptions: South Korea (AFC, 2002) and Morocco (CAF, 2022).

Champions, 1982–2022 (11 decided)

Only two confederations have ever won a World Cup in this span — indeed, ever. Europe took seven of eleven; South America the other four. 2026 is still open.

The fine print that changes the map

Nations that switched confederations

Confederation membership is not fixed. Where a team is credited depends on which body it qualified through in that cycle — and a few moves genuinely reshuffle the funnel.

Border-crossing associations

2006→
Australia left the OFC for the AFC on 1 Jan 2006. It qualified for 2006 via Oceania (beating Uruguay in a play-off) — credited here to OFC — but from 2010 through the AFC. This is the single most consequential switch in the dataset.
~1994
Israel, expelled from the AFC in 1974, spent years in the Oceania/European qualifying wilderness (it contested Oceania's play-off route as late as 1990 qualifying) before joining UEFA.
2002
Kazakhstan moved from the AFC to UEFA.
1991–93
Dissolutions stayed within UEFA: the USSR (→ Russia + others; Central-Asian republics went to the AFC), SFR Yugoslavia (→ Croatia, Slovenia…), and Czechoslovakia (→ Czechia + Slovakia). West Germany won 1990 as a divided nation; reunified that October.

Rules that move the numbers

  • Host auto-qualifies and is counted in the host's confederation — including co-hosts (Korea + Japan, both AFC, 2002) and three hosts (USA, Canada, Mexico, all CONCACAF, 2026).
  • Holders auto-qualified through 2002 (last: France). This padded CONMEBOL to five in 1998 (Brazil) and UEFA in 2002; abolished from 2006.
  • Inter-confederation play-offs decide the extra places — shown here as +1. They repeatedly settled whether OFC sent anyone at all, and in 2026 handed the two extra spots to CAF (DR Congo) and AFC (Iraq).
  • OFC had no guaranteed berth until 2026. Across 1982–2022 it reached the field only via play-offs — New Zealand (1982, 2010) and Australia (2006, as OFC).
Methodology & sources

How this was built

Each of the twelve editions was researched independently against primary references and cross-checked so that every ring sums correctly: qualified fields sum to 24 / 32 / 48; direct berths plus play-off +1s reconcile exactly to the field; each knockout tier halves cleanly (16 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1). Teams are attributed to the confederation they qualified through in that cycle, honouring the switches above. FIFA-membership totals for past editions are approximate (reconstructed from confederation admission dates and known milestones: ≈150 members in 1982, passing 200 around 1998–2000, and 211 since Kosovo & Gibraltar joined in 2016); the 2026 per-confederation breakdown — UEFA 55, CAF 54, AFC 46, CONCACAF 35, CONMEBOL 10, OFC 11 = 211 — is exact.
