The story the numbers tell

One pattern dominates all three tournaments: Germany generated far more chance quality (xG, xGOT) and territory (field tilt) than their opponents — and repeatedly failed to win the games that decided their fate. That is exactly what these metrics exist to expose: chronic finishing under-performance and vulnerability to clinical opponents. There are two exceptions across 12 games — the 2026 Ecuador defeat (genuinely out-played) and, territorially, the 2022 Spain draw (the only match Germany were dominated for field position — yet they still created the better chances).

📖 Metrics glossary

Expected Goals (xG)

The probability (0–1) that a shot is scored, from distance, angle, body part, assist type and whether it was a big chance — calibrated on ~1M+ historical shots. A team's match xG measures chance quality created and is more stable and predictive than raw goals.

🔗 The Analyst — What is xG · FBref model

Expected Goals on Target (xGOT / PSxG)

Computed only for on-target shots, adding where in the goal the ball was placed (off-target = 0). It isolates finishing/shot placement and, defensively, goalkeeping. Opta calls it xGOT; FBref/StatsBomb call the same idea PSxG.

🔗 The Analyst — What is xGOT

Field tilt

A team's share of the final-third passes (or touches) in a match: team's final-third passes ÷ both teams' total × 100. Unlike possession, it ignores sterile passing in defence/midfield and measures territorial dominance.

🔗 Driblab · The Football Analyst

Big chances

A situation where a player should reasonably be expected to score — usually a one-on-one or clear close-range opportunity (penalties always count). Tracks the volume of the highest-quality chances, complementing xG.

🔗 Opta event definitions · The Analyst glossary

Shots / Shots on target · Possession

Goal attempts (and the on-target subset) are volume indicators, predictive mainly in combination with xG. Possession is weakly predictive alone — Germany's three campaigns are the case study.

🔗 Opta event definitions

PPDA (not recovered)

Passes allowed Per Defensive Action — opponent passes ÷ the defending team's pressing actions in a zone; lower = more intense pressing. Not published per-match on any source accessed for these games, so it is absent from the tables.

⚠️ Source consistency & limitations

Your ideal — one consistent Opta source for every stat across all three World Cups — is now largely met, with the qualifications below. Read these before making cross-era comparisons.
  1. xG — Opta for all 12 matches, but from different Opta front-ends (no single free site spans 2018→2026): 2018 & 2022 from FBref (served by Opta) via Wayback Machine; 2022 & 2026 from FotMob and ESPN / The Analyst. 2022 overlaps both eras and the values agree — useful validation.
  2. xGOT — Opta for all 12 matches. 2018: FBref PSxG (keeper "PSxG faced"). 2022 & 2026: FotMob xGOT, each re-confirmed by summing the per-shot Opta xGOT in FotMob's shotmap (matched to the penny; shootout penalties excluded). xGOT (FotMob, 2 dp) and PSxG (FBref, 1 dp) are the same concept from two implementations and differ slightly.
  3. Field tilt — computed for all 12, not directly published. No source publishes a labelled "field tilt"; the figures are computed from Opta final-third passes (FBref for 2018/22; FotMob per-player sums for 2026). Caveats: FBref and FotMob may count "passes into the final third" slightly differently, so 2018/22 vs 2026 levels aren't perfectly comparable — trust within-tournament contrasts most. "Passes into the final third" counts entries, a close but imperfect proxy. Touch-based versions (parentheses) exist for 2018/22 only.
  4. 2018 provider caveat: FBref's 2018 xG was historically StatsBomb, but the late-2025 snapshots attribute it to Opta (FBref had migrated providers). So the 2018 numbers are Opta's retrospective values (consistent with 2022/26), not the StatsBomb figures reported at the time. No pre-migration snapshot exists to recover the originals.
  5. Why FBref needed the archive: On 20 January 2026, Stats Perform/Opta terminated FBref's data feed and FBref removed ALL advanced stats (incl. xG); live FBref also returns HTTP 403. The only way to get its Opta numbers today is via pre-20-Jan-2026 Wayback snapshots.
  6. Within-Opta variance: ESPN/The Analyst and FotMob are both Opta but differ slightly by rounding/implementation (2026 Curaçao xG 3.91 vs 4.22; Paraguay 1.49/0.42 vs 1.57/0.35). Primary xG shown is ESPN/The Analyst; FotMob in parentheses. 2026 xGOT is FotMob's — for a strict same-feed xG↔xGOT comparison, pair it with FotMob's (parenthesised) xG.
  7. Big chances: From FotMob/Opta (2022) and ESPN/Opta + Sofascore (2026). FBref doesn't display them, so 2018 big-chance counts are unavailable.
  8. PPDA: Not published per-match on any accessed source — genuinely unavailable here.
  9. 2026 is live / post-cutoff: All 2026 figures come from in-tournament match centres (ESPN, FotMob, The Analyst, Sofascore), not a settled archive — mutually consistent but newer than the assistant's training data.
Bottom line: You have a single consistent provider (Opta) for xG and xGOT across all 12 matches, and a consistent method for field tilt across all 12. The unavoidable compromises: no single website spans all three tournaments; field tilt is computed (not published) and mixes FBref/FotMob inputs across eras; 2018 values are Opta's retrospective model, not 2018-era StatsBomb; PPDA and 2018 big chances are unavailable. Everything in the tables is real and cited; nothing is estimated.
